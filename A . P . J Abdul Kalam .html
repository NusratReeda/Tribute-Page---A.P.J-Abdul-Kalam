A . P . J Abdul Kalam

1931-2015

"खुदा आपको वो सब हक़ीक़त में जीने की ताक़त दे, जो आपके सपनों में है।"

"May God give you the strength to live the reality of your dreams."

Biography

Introduction:

Avul Pakir Jainulabdeen Abdul Kalam, popularly known as APJ Abdul Kalam, was an Indian scientist and statesman who served as the 11th President of India from 2002 to 2007. He was born on October 15, 1931, in Rameswaram, Tamil Nadu, India, and passed away on July 27, 2015. Kalam was a prominent figure in India's space and missile programs and was often referred to as the "Missile Man of India" for his contributions to the development of ballistic missile technology.


Early Life and Education:

Kalam hailed from a modest background. His father was a boat owner and imam of a local mosque, while his mother was a housewife. Despite the family's financial constraints, Kalam was a bright student and showed a keen intrest in science and technology from a young age.

He pursued his education at the Schwartz Higher Secondary School in Ramanathapuram, followed by a degree in aeronautical engineering from the Madras Institute of Technology (now part of Anna University) in Chennai. He graduated in 1954 and later joined the Defense Research and Development Organization (DRDO) as a scientist.


Scientific Achievements:

Kalam's contributions to India's space and defense programs were instrumental. He played a pivotal role in the development of India's first indigenous satellite launch vehicle, SLV-3, which successfully launched the Rohini satellite into space in 1980. He also contributed to the development of ballistic missile technology, including the Agni and Prithvi missiles.

His work in defense technology earned him several awards and honors, including the Bharat Ratna, India's highest civilian award, in 1997. Kalam's leadership and vision were crucial in shaping India's space and defense capabilities.


Presidential Tenure:

In 2002, Kalam was elected as the President of India, succeeding K. R. Narayanan. His presidency was marked by his strong commitment to youth and education. He often spoke about the importance of science, technology, and education in shaping India's future. He envisioned a developed India by 2020, which he termed as the "Vision 2020."

Despite his political position, Kalam remained a scientist at heart and continued to interact with students and researchers, encouraging them to pursue their dreams and contribute to the nation's progress.


Legacy:

APJ Abdul Kalam's legacy extends beyond his scientific achievements and presidency. He was a beloved figure among Indians, especially the youth, who saw him as an inspiration. He authored several books, including "Wings of Fire," an autobiography, and "Ignited Minds," which emphasizes the importance of education and innovation in transforming India into a developed nation.

Kalam's humility, dedication, and vision have left an indelible mark on India's scientific, technological, and educational landscape. His birthday, October 15th, is celebrated as "World Students' Day" in his honor.


Conclusion:

APJ Abdul Kalam's life journey is a testament to the power of determination, hard work, and the pursuit of knowledge. He remains an inspiration to countless individuals, both in India and around the world, who aspire to make a positive impact through their contributions to science, technology, and society.
